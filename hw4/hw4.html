The Importance of Privacy

The most interesting quote to me from the article Long Live the Web is the paragraph about ISP snooping on network traffic;

"Other threats to the web result from meddling with the Inter-net, including snooping. In 2008 one company, Phorm, devised a way for an ISP to peek inside the packets of information it was sending. The ISP could determine every URI that any customer was browsing. The ISP could then create a profile of the sites the user went to in order to produce targeted advertising."

This information, in the wrong hands, is extremely dangerous to the continuation of a free world. Not only do ISPs do this, Google and Facebook do this. What exists currently is a method for these companies to develop a psychological profile about your interests and your personality, based off how you interact with the internet. "Oh you clicked x article, stayed on it for 30 seconds, and then viewed y article which oppposes x and stayed there for 15 minutes, you must be a supporter of y beliefs" (As a rudimentary example). This information may be okay in the hands of the all benevolent government of the country which you reside, however if you live in a tyrannical regime, you may not like if your government has this information.

This might seem like a crazy idea to you, living a free country, but why don't you ask how this turned out for the Uighar Muslims living in China. China essentially is rounding people up and putting them in concentration camps due to their religious beliefs. Methodologies of doing this include obvious things, like taking people from their places of worship, but other, more oppressive ways of doing this include tracking which websites people visit, assessing their affiliation with said information, and rounding them up after the results. This type of behavior on China's part is not only a crime against humanity, but it is a threat to the idea of a free internet, and a free world.

This information being tracked might not lead to concentration camps in the US today, but having the data exist in some way allows a potential future regime to use this data for malicious purposes.

If you want to combat some of these things, switch to Firefox and DuckDuckGo, I've described these in more depth on my homepage.
